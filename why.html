Why I Built FounderOS

Startups are the fun kind of chaos. The high-velocity, whiteboard-filled, scrappy kind. The kind that makes you fall in love with building.

But between the brainstorms and breakthroughs, there's the chaos that just... grinds you down.

The unglamorous kind — scattered W-9s, forgotten 1099s, surprise compliance fees, investor updates you meant to send last week.

I've felt that pressure firsthand — building internal tools at Seed, scaling ops at Lime and Built In, helping high-growth teams at Bumble stay on track.

Now I support 30+ startups in Cincinnati through venture — and one day I walked up to a founder friend in our portfolio who had literal piles of paper spread out around him. I asked what he was doing.

He just looked up and laughed: "Paperwork."

That moment stuck with me.

Because most founders aren't dropping the ball out of carelessness — they're just buried in things that shouldn't require their brainpower in the first place.

So I started paying closer attention. The patterns were everywhere:

  • Founders manually onboarding contractors
  • Paying late fees on filings they didn't know were due
  • Copy-pasting metrics into investor updates at 11pm
  • Winging operations because they didn't have time to build systems

I built FounderOS to quietly take care of all of that.

Not with another dashboard. Not with a todo list. But with an invisible operating layer that connects to the tools you already use — and just gets it done.

FounderOS handles the stuff that's not worth your time — but too risky to ignore.

No chaos. No checklists. No Sunday scaries.

Just calm momentum in the background.
